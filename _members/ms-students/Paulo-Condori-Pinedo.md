Master Student, Computer Science, Emory University

I am a master's student in Computer Science at Emory University interested in the intersection of artificial intelligence, human cognition, and human centered computing. My interests focus on understanding neurodivergent cognition and how differences in attention, learning, and information processing shape the way people interact with technology. I am particularly curious about questions such as why some people struggle with attention while others do not, and how emerging technologies might help us better understand and support these differences. I am interested in exploring how AI systems, wearable technologies, and computational models could assist in the early detection and support of conditions such as ADHD, dyslexia, autism, and other forms of neurodivergence, with the goal of developing adaptive technologies that better respond to diverse cognitive styles.
